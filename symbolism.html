Symbols

Welcome to our exploration of common Mongolian symbols.

өлзий хээ - Eternal Knot

The өлзий хээ, also known as the 'Eternal Knot,' symbolizes eternal love and unity across all realms. Often found adorning fences and integrated into architectural designs, it serves as a profound representation of enduring bonds and interconnectedness.

Соёмбо - (Soyombo)

The Соёмбо, Mongolia's national symbol, embodies rich cultural heritage and profound symbolism. Its components convey deep meanings: Flame: Flourishing family. Three prongs: Mongolian prosperity – past, present, future. Sun and crescent: Mongolian origins. Triangles: Defense of freedom and independence. Upper triangle triumphs over internal foes; lower represents victory over external adversaries. Rectangles: Honesty, justice, meritocracy. Yin-yang: Gender unity, once fish symbolizing vigilance, wisdom. Vertical rectangles: Fortress walls, symbolizing unity's strength. The Соёмбо is prominently featured on the Mongolian flag, as well as on government buildings and public spaces, serving as a powerful symbol of national identity and resilience.

хас тэмдэ - (khas temdeg)

The хас тэмдэ (khas temdeg), also known as the swastika, holds profound significance in Mongolian culture, dating back thousands of years. Revered as a symbol of good luck, prosperity, and auspiciousness, it is commonly displayed as a symbol of good fortune. You will commonly see this symbol on fences, patterns on clothing, and integrated in various artwork, embodying enduring cultural heritage and spiritual resonance.
