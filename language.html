Mongolian Language

Introduction to the Mongolian Language

Mongolian is the official language of Mongolia and is spoken by the majority of its population. It belongs to the Mongolic branch of the Altaic language family and is written using the Cyrillic script, introduced during the Soviet era. The language has a rich history and cultural significance, with various dialects spoken across different regions of Mongolia.

Characteristics of the Mongolian Language

Mongolian is known for its vowel harmony system, where vowels in a word must harmonize with each other in terms of their frontness or backness. The language also has a complex system of case marking, which indicates grammatical relationships within sentences. Additionally, Mongolian has borrowed vocabulary from other languages over time, particularly from Tibetan, Sanskrit, Russian, and Chinese.

Importance of the Mongolian Language

The Mongolian language plays a vital role in preserving the cultural heritage and identity of the Mongolian people. It is not only a means of communication but also a symbol of national pride. Learning Mongolian can enhance cultural understanding and facilitate deeper connections with the people and traditions of Mongolia.

10 Essential Phrases for Travelers

  • Hello / Hi:
    Cyrillic: Сайн уу (Sain uu)
    Romanized: Sain uu
  • Thank you:
    Cyrillic: Баярлалаа (Bayarlalaa)
    Romanized: Bayarlalaa
  • Yes:
    Cyrillic: Тийм (Tiim)
    Romanized: Tiim
  • No:
    Cyrillic: Үгүй (Ugui)
    Romanized: Ugui
  • Excuse me / Sorry:
    Cyrillic: Уучлаарай (Uuchlaarai)
    Romanized: Uuchlaarai
  • Where is...?:
    Cyrillic: Хаана байдаг вэ? (Khaana baidag ve?)
    Romanized: Khaana baidag ve?
  • How much is this?:
    Cyrillic: Энэ хэдэн вэ? (Ene kheden ve?)
    Romanized: Ene kheden ve?
